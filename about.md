Hi! I am a Master's Mechanical Engineering student at Northeastern University with a strong interest in designing thoughtful and functional products. My passion for engineering originates from a fascination with understanding how different devices work behind the scenes and finding creative solutions to solve unique problems.

Through my academics and co-op experiences at **SharkNinja** and **Instron**, I've developed a widespread technical skill set ranging from product design, prototyping, and problem solving.

Looking forward, I’m excited about exploring mechanical design where I can apply both my technical skills and creative problem-solving abilities.

Outside of academics, I enjoy working on personal projects like 3D printing and building mechanical keyboards. These hobbies have allowed me to experiment with design and prototyping in my own time.

## My Experience

At **Instron**, I worked with the R&D team and spent most of my time helping to develop an authoinjector testing system. This gave me insight on designing for manufacturing, technical documentation, and the precision required in mechanical design. This experience also taught me how to look at the bigger picture when trying to solve problems.

My time at **SharkNinja** on the Heated Products NPD team gave me a different perspective: working on consumer products, specifically air fryers, ovens, and grills. I gained appreciation for the amount of work and thought that goes into products people interact with on a daily basis - the magnitude of design decisions that the user should never need to think about.

For my senior capstone, I collaborated on designing an insect capture device that won the "Best Iterative Design" award. This project reinforced what I enjoy the most about engineering: taking an idea and shaping it into something you're proud of.

## What I'm Looking For

I'm graduating in **May 2026** and looking for mechanical engineering roles where I can apply my R&D and mechanical design experience in product development, electromechanical systems design, or consumer products. I'm especially interested in work that involves making thoughtful things that have a meaningful difference.

I'm based in Boston and open to opportunities around New England.
